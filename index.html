HOW WE WORK

From breadboard to finished build

01 — Define the constraints

Power budget, size, environment, and the one thing the device absolutely must never do wrong.

02 — Prototype on the bench

Breadboard or dev-board first. We find the hard problems early, while they're still cheap to fix.

03 — Design the real board

Schematic, layout, and a first fabrication run — sized and routed for the final enclosure.

04 — Bring-up and stress test

Power it on, push it past normal operating conditions, and fix what breaks before the client sees it.

SELECTED WORK

Projects

A running log of what we've shipped — from research-grade rovers to household companion robots.

01

Mobile Autonomous Responsive Vehicle (M.A.R.V.)

An autonomous companion robot built on a localized ROS2 framework spanning NVIDIA Jetson and Raspberry Pi infrastructure. MARV runs a quantized Qwen2.5:3B model for everyday conversational interaction, a YOLO-based vision pipeline for fast object and person detection paired with 360° LiDAR for ranging and mapping, and a full auditory pipeline — Whisper for speech recognition and a trained Piper model for natural, human-like spoken responses.

02

High-sensitivity Unmanned Magnetic Mapping Electric Rover (H.U.M.M.E.R.)

A semi-autonomous rover built in a class setting to detect and map ferrous materials underground, in response to a real-world problem of unknown underground piping. Controlled by an Arduino and Pixhawk and fitted with an FVM-400 fluxgate magnetometer, the rover successfully located the North and South magnetic poles of 20m-long steam pipes buried 8–14 feet deep. Role: developed the software backend integrating Raspberry Pis to interface with, stream, and collect magnetometer data, then built the post-collection analysis tools — raw data processing and satellite GPS mapping of pipe locations.

03

Engraved + NFC business cards

Anodized aluminum cards, laser-engraved and embedded with an NFC chip that opens a custom link-in-bio page on tap.
